About Me

I am a doctoral student at School of Environment, Tsinghua University, specializing in traffic emissions and air quality modeling. My research uses CMAQ and other atmospheric chemistry models to investigate the sources, transformation, and transport of atmospheric pollutants.

I received my B.Eng. from the College of Environmental Science and Engineering at Nankai University (2021–2025). Before that, I attended the Foreign Language School attached to Guangxi Normal University (2015–2021).

My research interests include air quality numerical modeling, emission inventories, atmospheric chemistry, urban dispersion modeling, and machine learning for environmental applications. I work with tools including WRF, CMAQ, PALM-4U, RLINE, and Python/R for data analysis.

At Nankai, I served as President of the Environmental Science Association (2023–2024) and as a teaching assistant for Organic Chemistry for three consecutive years (2023–2025), earning the Outstanding Teaching Assistant Award. I received the Outstanding Graduate Award, the Dai Shugui Environmental Chemistry Scholarship, and the Tianjin Municipal Government Scholarship. I also won first prize and second prize in the 34th and 33rd Chinese Chemistry Olympiad (Preliminary Round), respectively.
